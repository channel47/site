005 ECOSYSTEM

Three Tools, Three Jobs

MCP Servers
CONNECTORS
The wiring. Connects AI to external data and tools.
Think of it as the power outlets in the room.
Sub-Agents
THE TEAM
AI delegating tasks to other AI. A project manager assigning work.
Think of it as a PM distributing tasks.
Skills
THE PLAYBOOK
Instructions that tell AI how to do a specific job, step by step, every time.
Think of it as your best SOP, written for AI.
MCP is the wiring. Sub-agents are the team. Skills are the playbook.

011 ANATOMY

Anatomy of a SKILL.md

--- name: campaign-builder description: "Generates a full Google Ads search campaign from a landing page URL" --- # Context You are a performance marketer. The user will give you a URL. Analyze the page and build a search campaign. # Steps 1. Extract the page's value proposition 2. Identify target keywords (3 ad groups) 3. Write 3 responsive search ads per group 4. Suggest negative keywords 5. Set recommended daily budget # What to Avoid - Generic headlines ("Best Solution") - More than 30 characters per headline - Broad match without negatives # Example Output [structured campaign output...]
That's it. Plain language at the core. Skills can also include reference files, templates, and scripts alongside the SKILL.md.
012 PERSONAL vs. GENERIC

The Best Skills Are Personal

GENERIC
"Write Google Ads copy."
PERSONAL
"Write Google Ads copy for my SaaS product that targets mid-market CFOs, uses our specific value props, avoids competitor terms we've tested, and follows our brand voice."
Your brand voice
Your audience
Your positioning
The most useful skills aren't templates. They're your playbook.
013 WATCH FOR

Common Skill Mistakes

Vague descriptions
The skill never triggers. AI doesn't know when to load it.
Overlong instructions
AI loses focus. Competing directives. Muddled output.
Be specific in the description, concise in the instructions.
014 CLARITY

What Skills Are NOT

Not integrations
Connecting AI to tools is a different layer. Skills are pure instructions — no wiring required.
Not AI managing other AI
That's sub-agents. Skills are instructions, not orchestration.
Not saved prompts
A prompt disappears after one chat. A skill persists, improves, and works across conversations.
Not autopilot
A skill guides the work. It doesn't replace your judgment on the output.
A skill is a playbook, not the player.
